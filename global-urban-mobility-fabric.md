# Global Urban Mobility Fabric

**Open infrastructure for city mobility — a program by Networks for Humanity and World Resources Institute**

---

## The Problem

Cities are investing in mobility. New modes, new business models, new technologies — shared mobility, ride-hailing, micro-mobility, last-mile delivery, electric, autonomous, urban aviation. But every investment is building its own world. The traveller is left to connect them alone.

The result: a city of silos, where value is locked inside individual systems and every new connection has to start from scratch.

---

## This Shift Has Happened Before

Three networks changed the world by building a shared open layer:

- **The Internet** — did not build websites or online commerce. It built the open standard that let anyone build anything and reach anyone.
- **GPS** — did not build navigation apps. It built the positioning layer that maps, logistics, and ride-hail all grew from.
- **Mobile Networks** — did not build services. They built the universal standard that let any device, any provider, connect to any other.

The Global Urban Mobility Fabric brings this shift to cities. The infrastructure of connections.

---

## What It Is

A shared open standard — owned by no one, open to everyone — that enables any mobility platform or service to discover, negotiate, and transact value across city mobility.

**Four principles:**

1. **Join once.** A single connection makes any mobility service immediately reachable by every other service on the network.
2. **Reach everything.** New services become available across the entire ecosystem the moment they join.
3. **Grow beyond mobility.** As more services join, the city's mobility layer becomes part of a broader network of commerce, logistics, energy, and more.
4. **Nothing in the middle.** Like TCP/IP, it sits beneath every connection it enables — a standard, not a platform.

---

## The Complete Stack

The Fabric sits within a full open infrastructure from Networks for Humanity:

- **Mobility Web** — the connection layer. Any mobility service joins once and reaches all others.
- **Vouch Finance** — purpose-bound value. Money, credit, or equity issued with embedded rules — directed to the right recipient, usable in the right context, tracked to the right outcome.
- **Pincer** — cryptographic trust. A seal that enforces itself and settles the moment proof conditions are met. No intermediary, no delay.

## What It Enables

**For the individual:** A commuter plans a journey in one place — combining micro-mobility, transit, and ride-hail across three separate operators — booked and paid in one flow. Every operator is paid when their leg completes. If a leg fails, that portion returns automatically.

**For organisations:** An employer issues a mobility allowance through Vouch — purpose-bound credit spendable across any connected service, automatically restricted to sustainable modes. Mobility Web verifies the full journey, not just the payment. Carbon impact is measured from actual trips, not surveys. Climate commitments become measurable.

**For neighbourhoods:** Idle parking spaces publish real-time availability — becoming micro-mobility hubs by morning and EV charging depots by night. School buses and corporate coaches sitting idle for most of the day are listed once and discoverable by any transit operator. Route gaps close using vehicles that already exist.

**For cities:** Transit workforce credentials are verified and stored on the Fabric — portable across every employer and city. Cities planning electric fleets can see whether the talent to drive them exists before the vehicles arrive. EV charging demand is built on real data, not estimates. Congestion incentives are tied to verified behaviour — the budget pays only when Mobility Web confirms a mode switch happened.

## What It Enables That Nobody Has Predicted

Open layers create conditions for solutions nobody designed — including AI-native interfaces that discover, transact, and coordinate across a city in real time. Every new service that joins increases the value for every other service already connected. The open standard gets more powerful the more it is used.

## Who Built It

**Networks for Humanity (NFH)** is a global non-profit initiative providing open, interoperable, and decentralised digital infrastructure that enables broader access and participation in the digital economy. The NFH Fabric powers the Global Urban Mobility Fabric, providing the open standard any mobility service can connect to.

**World Resources Institute (WRI)** is a global research organisation working to improve people's lives, protect nature, and stabilise the climate. WRI brings city relationships, policy expertise, and on-the-ground research that makes the Fabric work in practice.
